REVIEW: Windsor at Cambridge — Cambridge, MA
Rating: 3/5 stars
Reviewer: Former resident, 2021-2022

Windsor is a larger professionally-managed complex near MIT. Pros: in-unit laundry, modern appliances, responsive maintenance portal. Cons: expensive ($2,600/month for a 1-bed), thin walls, and the building has a lot of turnover so it doesn't feel like a community. For grad students on a stipend it's usually too expensive unless you split a 2-bed. The management team was professional and the deposit was returned in full.

REVIEW: Somerville triple-decker, Davis Square area
Rating: 4/5 stars
Reviewer: Current tenant, private listing found on Craigslist

Renting from a local landlord who owns a triple-decker near Davis Square. $1,300/month per person in a 3-bed. The building is old (1910s) but the unit was renovated — new kitchen, hardwood floors. Heating is oil-fired and we pay separately which can add $200-300/month in winter. The landlord is an older guy who is slow to respond by text but always comes through. Fixed a roof leak within a week. Has not raised rent in 2 years. This is the kind of Boston rental experience that's hard to find.

REVIEW: Avalon at Assembly Row — Somerville
Rating: 2/5 stars
Reviewer: Former resident 2022-2023

Assembly Row is a nice area but Avalon is a corporate landlord and it shows. They raised rent $400/month at renewal (about 20%). When you call with a problem you get a call center, not a property manager. Maintenance requests go into an app and sometimes sit for a week before anyone responds. The amenities (gym, roof deck) are nice but you're paying for them whether you use them or not. For students, this is not the right landlord — the lease terms are rigid, no subletting, and they have no flexibility for co-op situations.

REVIEW: Local landlord — Inman Square, Cambridge
Rating: 5/5 stars
Reviewer: Current tenant, found through MIT housing board

This is my third year in this apartment and I have no plans to leave. The landlord is a local family that has owned the building for 30 years. Super responsive — I've never waited more than 2 days for a maintenance response. They do annual inspections and proactively fixed things I hadn't even noticed. Rent is $1,450/month for my room in a 3-bed. They raised it $50 after year two. By Cambridge standards this is incredibly fair. The neighborhood is great — walkable to Central Square, easy bike ride to MIT.

REVIEW: Medford Street apartment near Ball Square, Somerville
Rating: 3/5 stars
Reviewer: Tufts grad student, 2023

Good location for Tufts — 15 minute walk to campus. The apartment itself was fine, older building but maintained. Main issue was the heating was unreliable — the steam radiators would clank and hiss all night in winter and the heat wasn't always consistent room to room. Landlord was aware and said it was an old system. Not a dealbreaker but annoying. Rent was $1,100/month which is good for Somerville. Would rent from them again if the heat issue was fixed.

REVIEW: Property management company near Harvard Square, Cambridge
Rating: 1/5 stars
Reviewer: Former tenant 2020-2021

Professional management company, so you'd think they'd be better. They were not. Security deposit dispute was awful — they charged $800 for "repainting" an apartment that was already showing wear when we moved in and had photos to prove it. It took 3 months and a certified letter threatening legal action to get the money back. They also had the apartment listed for new tenants two weeks before our lease ended without telling us. Know your rights: in Massachusetts, landlords must return the security deposit within 30 days of move-out with an itemized list. If they don't, you can sue for 3x the deposit amount.
