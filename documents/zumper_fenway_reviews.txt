REVIEW: Building on Boylston Street, Fenway area
Rating: 4/5
Tenant review:
Lived here for a year as a Northeastern student. The building is well-maintained and management is responsive. Location is great — 10 minute walk to NEU, close to the Green Line, lots of restaurants and bars nearby. Rent was $1,550/month for my room in a 2-bed which is high but the convenience factor is real. The main downsides: loud on game nights (Red Sox stadium is nearby), parking doesn't exist, and the walls are thin. In-unit laundry was a nice bonus.

REVIEW: Apartment complex near Park Drive, Fenway
Rating: 3/5
Tenant review:
The management company is large and corporate — you feel like a number. Maintenance requests get submitted through an app and are usually handled within 5-7 business days for non-urgent things. Had a leak in the bathroom that took 2 weeks to fix because they kept saying a contractor was coming and canceling. The apartment itself was nice when we moved in. Location is good. At renewal they raised rent $250/month — about 15% — which felt aggressive. We negotiated down to $150 increase but it was a hassle.

REVIEW: Triple-decker on Tremont Street, Mission Hill
Rating: 5/5
Tenant review:
Small local landlord owns the building. He lives nearby and treats the building like it's his own because it basically is. Maintenance issues get fixed fast — I've had two things go wrong in 18 months and both were fixed within 2 days. He's reasonable about things like letting us paint a wall or hang pictures. Rent is $1,150/month which is very fair for the area. When I renewed he gave me a 30 day notice of a $60 increase and that was it. No drama. This is what renting should be like.

REVIEW: Apartment on Wigglesworth Street, Mission Hill (near NEU)
Rating: 2/5
Tenant review:
The location is convenient to Northeastern — 10 minute walk to campus. But the building had problems. Cockroach infestation in the kitchen that took 3 treatments over 4 months to resolve. The management was responsive to calls but the pest control company they used wasn't effective. Heating was inconsistent — some rooms warmer than others. The old radiator system clanked loudly at night. Rent was $1,100/month which I think was fair for Mission Hill but I wouldn't recommend this specific building. Ask current tenants about pests before you sign.

REVIEW: Newer apartment building near Ruggles Station, Roxbury Crossing
Rating: 4/5
Tenant review:
Relatively new construction, so the appliances and systems work. Managed by a mid-size company that is professional if not personal. They use an online portal for maintenance which I actually like — you get a confirmation number and can track status. Response time is usually 3-5 business days. Rent is $1,200/month for my room in a 3-bed. The neighborhood around Ruggles is developing — there's a Whole Foods now which tells you something. Two Orange Line stops to Northeastern takes 5 minutes. Good value.

REVIEW: Building on Calumet Street, Mission Hill
Rating: 3/5
Tenant review:
Classic Boston student apartment. Old building, not fancy, but the price is right at $1,050/month. The landlord is a local guy who owns a few buildings in the neighborhood. He's not particularly responsive but he does eventually fix things — took 10 days to fix a broken lock which is too long for a security issue. The apartment had the original 1970s kitchen but it works. Heat is included which saves real money in winter. I'd live there again for the price, but go in with appropriate expectations.
