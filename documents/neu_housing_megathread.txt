NEU OFF-CAMPUS HOUSING GUIDE (Community-maintained)
Last updated by community moderators

---

NEIGHBORHOODS NEAR NORTHEASTERN

Mission Hill
- Distance to campus: 5-15 minute walk depending on where you are
- Average rent (2024): $1,100-1,300/month per person in a 3-4 bed
- Vibe: Mixed residential neighborhood, many students, some long-term residents
- Safety: Generally fine near Huntington Ave and the main streets. More variable on side streets. Trust your instincts.
- Streets popular with NEU students: Calumet St, Cliff St, Wigglesworth St, Pontiac St
- Notes: No car needed. Street parking basically impossible. Many buildings are older triple-deckers. Heat often not included — ask before signing.

Roxbury Crossing
- Distance to campus: 2 Orange Line stops (5 minutes)
- Average rent: $950-1,150/month per person
- Vibe: More residential, less student-heavy than Mission Hill
- Safety: Improving but still mixed. Stick to the main streets.
- Notes: Cheapest option close to NEU. Good value if you're comfortable with the area.

Jamaica Plain
- Distance to campus: 15-20 min on Orange Line
- Average rent: $1,050-1,300/month per person
- Vibe: Diverse, community-oriented, great food on Centre Street
- Safety: Generally safe, especially near the Orange Line stops
- Notes: Popular with grad students and older co-op students. More of a grown-up neighborhood. Great if you want to feel less like you're in a college bubble.

Fenway
- Distance to campus: 5-10 minute walk
- Average rent: $1,400-1,800/month per person
- Vibe: Right next to campus, very convenient, good nightlife
- Safety: Generally safe, well-lit area
- Notes: Most expensive option near NEU. Worth it for the convenience if budget allows.

South End
- Distance to campus: 10-20 min walk or 1 T stop
- Average rent: $1,600-2,200/month per person
- Vibe: Upscale, beautiful brownstones, good restaurants
- Safety: Very safe
- Notes: Expensive but a great quality of life. More for students with higher budgets.

LEASE RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

1. No written move-in inspection form — if a landlord won't do a written move-in inspection, that's a sign they plan to dispute the security deposit. Insist on one or photograph everything with timestamped photos.

2. Heat and utilities not specified — Massachusetts leases must specify who pays heat. If it's not written in the lease, ask and get it in writing. "Oh don't worry, it's included" verbal promises are worthless.

3. "No subletting" clause — this is a dealbreaker for co-op students who will be leaving for 6 months. Make sure your lease allows subletting or you'll be paying rent on an empty apartment during co-op.

4. Vague damage clauses — phrases like "tenant responsible for any damage beyond normal wear and tear" are fine, but be suspicious of leases that define "normal wear and tear" very narrowly.

5. No pets allowed and you have a pet — this sounds obvious but read it. Some landlords don't care, others will use it as grounds for eviction.

6. Required renters insurance — not necessarily bad, but you need to budget for it. Usually $15-25/month.

---

AVERAGE UPFRONT COSTS TO BUDGET FOR

Standard Boston/Cambridge lease requires:
- First month's rent
- Last month's rent
- Security deposit (up to 1 month's rent under MA law)
- Broker fee: 0 to 1 month's rent (common in Boston, try to find no-fee listings)

Example: $1,200/month apartment with broker fee = $4,800 upfront before you move in.
