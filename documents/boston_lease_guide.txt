BOSTON OFF-CAMPUS HOUSING GUIDE FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS

THE SEPTEMBER 1ST PROBLEM

Boston is unique in the entire country for having a near-universal September 1st lease start date. The vast majority of apartments in Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, and Brookline turn over on September 1st. This creates several problems for students:

1. Competition: Everyone is looking at the same time. Good apartments get snapped up in February-March for a September 1st start. If you wait until May or June, you're choosing from the leftovers.

2. Co-op and semester mismatches: If you need to move in January for a spring semester or co-op start, you'll be subletting, month-to-month (expensive), or paying rent on an empty apartment from September.

3. Moving day chaos: September 1st moving day in Allston (called "Allston Christmas") is a city-wide event. Every moving truck in Greater Boston is booked. Moving costs spike. The streets are jammed. Budget extra and book movers as early as possible — some people book 3-4 months ahead.

UNDERSTANDING BOSTON UPFRONT COSTS

Massachusetts law allows landlords to collect:
- First month's rent
- Last month's rent
- Security deposit (maximum 1 month's rent)
- Broker fee (paid to the real estate broker, typically 1 month's rent)

Total upfront: 3-4 months of rent before you move in one stick of furniture.

For a $1,200/month apartment: $3,600 (no broker) to $4,800 (with broker) upfront.

Important notes:
- Security deposit must be held in a separate interest-bearing account
- Landlord must provide receipt for security deposit within 30 days
- Security deposit must be returned within 30 days of move-out with itemized deductions
- If landlord fails to follow these rules, you may be entitled to 3x the security deposit

HEAT AND UTILITIES: READ YOUR LEASE CAREFULLY

Many Boston apartments do NOT include heat. This is crucial to understand:

- Gas heat: You pay the gas bill directly. In an old, poorly-insulated triple-decker, this can be $150-400/month in winter.
- Oil heat: Less common but expensive. Some buildings share oil heating costs among units.
- Electric baseboard heat: The most expensive option. Can be $300-500/month in a poorly-insulated apartment.
- Hot water: Often a separate utility from heat. Ask specifically.
- "Heat included": Some apartments do include heat. These are worth a premium.

What to ask before signing: "Who pays for heat? Hot water? Electricity? Gas for cooking?" Get the answers in writing in the lease.

THE BROKER FEE SITUATION

A broker fee is a payment to the real estate agent who listed the apartment. In most cities, the landlord pays this. In Boston, tenants traditionally pay it.

Typical broker fee: 1 month's rent. On a $1,400/month apartment = $1,400 fee to an agent you may have met once.

How to avoid it:
- Search Craigslist — many no-fee apartments are listed there by owners directly
- Look for "no fee" listings on Zillow and Apartments.com
- Ask building owners directly if they manage their own units
- Note: Some newer rules in Massachusetts are changing this, but the market still often defaults to tenant-paid fees

SECURITY DEPOSIT PROTECTION

Document your apartment at move-in. This is the most important thing you can do:
1. Do a written move-in inspection with your landlord and keep a copy
2. Take video and photo of every room, wall, floor, appliance, and fixture
3. Note existing damage in writing and get landlord acknowledgment
4. Store photos somewhere you can access them a year later (cloud storage)

This documentation is your protection if the landlord tries to withhold your deposit for pre-existing damage.

---

LEASE RED FLAGS

- No written move-in inspection offered
- Vague language about who pays utilities
- No subletting allowed (problem for co-op students)
- Landlord won't put verbal promises in writing
- No lease at all (month-to-month verbal — extremely risky)
- Security deposit over 1 month's rent (illegal in Massachusetts)
- Landlord asks for cash only

BOSTON STUDENT NEIGHBORHOODS QUICK REFERENCE

Budget tier ($950-1,200/person): Roxbury Crossing, parts of Dorchester, parts of Jamaica Plain
Mid tier ($1,150-1,450/person): Mission Hill, Allston, Brighton, Somerville (farther from Davis)
Higher tier ($1,400-1,700/person): Fenway, Davis Square, Cambridge (non-Harvard)
Expensive ($1,700+/person): South End, Beacon Hill, Back Bay, Harvard Square area
